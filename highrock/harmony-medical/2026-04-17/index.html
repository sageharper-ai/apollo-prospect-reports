Executive Summary

Scope note — read first

Harmony Medical is a national SaaS platform, not a local medical practice. Baltimore County research reflects the visibility Harmony has in its own backyard — a useful baseline, but not representative of their actual addressable market, which is every medical practice in the U.S.

Two honest truths from this engagement: (1) at the Baltimore County level, Harmony has effectively zero local keyword demand — all 18 submitted keywords returned 0 SV. That's not a tooling failure; it's the reality that EHR/billing software buyers don't geo-qualify their searches by city+state. (2) National keyword research is the strategically-correct next step, and is outside the scope of a $75 county-baseline engagement (see Next Steps).

What this deliverable establishes

  • The search demand ladder tells the whole story: 250 SV/mo across 13 general-tier service-category keywords (EHR / billing / RCM / practice management), collapsing to 10 SV at semi-local ("baltimore"), and 0 SV at hyper-local ("baltimore md"). The staircase IS the finding — EHR/billing/RCM buyers do not geo-qualify their searches.
  • Competitive gap at the platform level: three peer SaaS platforms (Tebra, athenahealth, eClinicalWorks) have 50×–1,000× Harmony's domain authority, 100×–1,500× the backlinks, and near-perfect LLM visibility.
  • Brand strength: Harmony ranks #1 for its own branded queries and has a clean link profile (Spam Score 1, 212 referring domains). The foundation is sound; the gap is content and authority inventory, not strategy.
  • Site Health partial: harmonymedical.net returns 403 to Apollo's crawler (and likely to other audit tools). This itself is a finding — it affects how the site can self-diagnose issues.
The evidence for a national strategy, in one number

250 monthly searches. That's Harmony's real service-category demand at the Baltimore County filter once the geo modifier is removed — "ehr software," "medical billing software," "revenue cycle management," and 10 more. Lift the filter entirely and it's thousands-to-hundreds-of-thousands per month. Hyper-local "ehr software baltimore md" = 0. The $75 baseline proves what market Harmony is actually in; the $297 AIO Research upsell opens the door to competing in it.

The one honest constraint

Harmony cannot close the authority gap to Tebra / athenahealth / eClinicalWorks on organic alone in under 18 months. Reaching page 1 for "ehr software for small practices" requires sustained content production + backlink acquisition + possibly paid search to validate landing pages. This is a program, not a tweak.

Key takeaway

Harmony is competing in a space where the top 3 SaaS peers have 50×–1,000× the domain authority, 100×–1,500× the backlinks, and near-perfect LLM visibility. Closing any of these gaps is a multi-year effort measured in hundreds of thousands to millions of dollars in content + backlink investment. A $75 research product cannot substitute for that — but it clarifies where to invest next. Harmony's one Perplexity hit (1 topic / 20% visibility) is an opportunity base to build from.

Strengths

  • Harmony ranks #1 for its own brand queries (harmony médical SV 170, harmony medical SV 480, harmony emr SV 30) — strong branded visibility.
  • Spam Score of 1 — clean link profile.
  • 212 referring domains for a specialty SaaS is reasonable baseline.
  • Some Perplexity LLM visibility (1 topic, 20%) — not zero.

Weaknesses

  • DR 4 / DA 20 relative to peers in the 50–85 range means Harmony is effectively invisible in organic search for non-branded EHR queries.
  • Zero paid activity (0 paid keywords, 0 paid traffic) means no demand capture on high-intent searches right now.
  • ~40% of organic traffic appears to be French-language ("harmony médical") — possibly overseas / confused searchers, not U.S. buyers.
  • LLM visibility near-zero on 5 of 6 platforms means AI-mediated shopping flows (increasingly significant for B2B SaaS) are routing around Harmony.

Site Health

Status: selective crawler block (not universal)

harmonymedical.net returned 403 Forbidden to Apollo's crawler on 2026-04-16, which initially looked like a universal audit block. Verified via Google PageSpeed Insights on 2026-04-17: PSI ran clean on both mobile and desktop — so the 403 is a selective filter (likely user-agent or IP-range specific), not a universal block. Common SEO tools with rotating IPs or recognized user-agents (SEMrush, Ahrefs, Screaming Frog) may or may not be blocked depending on Harmony's filter rules.

Recommendation: Whitelist Apollo's crawler user-agent for future audits. The filter as configured is not obviously malicious — it's likely a default security posture from a WAF or CDN — but it does create blind spots in on-page SEO audits. See PSI data in Competitive Landscape for what is reachable.

Methodology notes

  • City-phrased keywords ("baltimore md") with a Baltimore County, MD geographic filter. County names are never searched directly; city names are.
  • US-wide data was not used and is not used in any Apollo SEO deliverable — it inflates expectations and breaks client trust.
  • All 4 domains were pulled through an authority-data lookup that avoids the caching issues of managed-project views, so backlink counts reflect what public-facing audit tools would also see.
  • Any metric that returned null, zero-as-artifact, or was unavailable at extraction time is flagged INCOMPLETE rather than presented as fact.
  • 0 SV for a correctly-phrased local keyword IS real data, not a bug. For a national SaaS, 0 SV on 18 county-filtered queries is the expected and honest result.
